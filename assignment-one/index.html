Welcome

My first assignment of second term.

Second semester is finally here, which means I only have 3/4 of the program to go. Yay.

I was going to write about my Christmas Break but I realize the deep intricacies of visiting family and eating a reprehensible amount of food may not be the most exciting thing for most people, so I'll just stick with some highlights.

I travelled back home to Cape Breton, and it was nice to stick around for almost a whole month instead of my usual weekend trips I take throughout the year. As much as I would like to regale you with the best of the Island, my hometown of Sydney Mines is like New York—not that it's the city that never sleeps, but rather the town that only sleeps.

I guess the most exciting thing that happened was that I turned 20—and just my luck, I was welcomed into a new decade of living with a lovely thing called the Norovirus. I was out of comission for around 36 hours but I didn't mind since I would've spent it on my ass anyway.

When I got back to Halifax I figured I'd spruce up my room with some new decor so that my oil pastel drawing of Danny DeVito didn't get too lonely. I bought some wall art, some plants, and a rug, turning what was once an average room into what I like to call the "Ultimate White Boy Hangout." It's a title I boast with pride.
